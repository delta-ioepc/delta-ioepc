Rules

  1. Teams can involve at least 2 team members and a maximum of 4 members.
  2. Participants must bring their school or college identity card with them.
  3. All team members should be present at the event. Leaving the prescribed venue will disqualify the entire team from Deltathon.
  4. Teams can, of course, gain advice and support from organizers, volunteers, sponsors, and others.
  5. All work on a project should be done during the Deltathon.
  6. Teams can use an idea they had before the event.
  7. No development may start before the actual date and time of the event. Any teams that violates this rule will be automatically disqualified.
  8. Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects with proper references mentioned.
  9. Teams must stop hacking once the time is up.
  10. You must treat all team members, including competitors, judges, coaches, volunteers, etc., with respect and courtesy.
  11. All team members should be a regular student studying at +2, Bacholer or Master's level in any discipline.
  12. All prizes are to be shared between all team members.
  13. Participants can consult with a mentor only if they are stuck implementing the core code of their field, not to nurture their ideas.
  14. Participants must face the consequences if any suspected activities occur.
  15. In the event of a disagreement, the organizing committee makes the final decision.

Code of Conduct

  1. Our hackathon is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, previous hackathon attendance, computing experience (or lack of any of the aforementioned). We do not tolerate harassment of hackathon participants in any form. Hackathon participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the hackathon at the discretion of the organizers
  2. This includes warning the offender, expulsion from the hackathon with no refund (if applicable), or reporting their behavior to local law enforcement. We expect participants to follow these rules at hackathon and workshop venues and hackathon related social events.
  3. Participants must not smoke, drink, or perform any unethical behavior like quarreling that will harm the integrity of this event. In the event of an encounter, strict action will be taken.
